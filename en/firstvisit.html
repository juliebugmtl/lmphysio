The First Visit

Here are some tips to facilitate your first physiotherapy session (the assessment):

  • It is recommended to wear or bring ample and comfortable clothes to make sure the physiotherapist can have access to the area that needs to be evaluated (eg: shorts for a knee problem, tank top/short sleeved t-shirt for a shoulder problem).
  • Although it is not mandatory to see a physician before coming to physiotherapy, if you have any, bring all medical documents related to your problem (doctor's prescription, X-ray or MRI results, etc.).
  • Upon arrival, you will need to fill out a form describing your lifestyle, medical history (injuries, hospitalizations) and other medical conditions so that the physiotherapist has a good idea of ​​your overall health. Try to arrive 15 minutes in advance to complete these forms.
  • The first session will be mainly devoted to the evaluation of the problem by means of questions and tests in order to clearly identify the cause of the problem and to list the limitations to be worked on. With this information, the physiotherapist will be able to establish a treatment plan in line with your goals and will begin to treat you the same day. Several sessions may be necessary depending on the severity of the problem.
  • At the end of the visit, a receipt will be given to you so that you can make a claim with your private insurance. Please note that CSST and SAAQ patients are not accepted.
