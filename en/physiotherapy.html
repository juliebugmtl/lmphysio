Physiotherapy

Definition: The physiotherapist is a health professional who works with the population of all ages with musculoskeletal and neurological disorders. The health problems that can be treated in physiotherapy are, for example:

By joint

  • Back (eg: back pain, herniated discs, sciatica)
  • Knee (eg: patellofemoral syndrome, patellar tendinopathy, ilio-tibial band syndrome, sprain)
  • Foot or Ankle (eg: tendinopathy of the Achilles tendon, plantar fascitis, sprain)
  • Neck (eg: neck pain, migraine, torticolis)
  • Shoulder (eg: rotator cuff injury, post-shoulder dislocation, capsulitis or frozen shoulder, impingement syndrome, instability problem)
  • Elbow (eg: medial and lateral tendinopathy, tennis elbow and golfer's elbow)
  • Wrist or Hand: Carpal tunnel syndrome


Other neuro-musculoskeletal problems

  • Post-surgery rehabilitation (eg: fracture, ligament, tendon or muscle rupture)
  • Sports injuries
  • Postural syndrome (eg: pain related to office work)
  • Arthritis, osteoarthritis
  • Ligament sprain
  • Tendinopathy (tendinitis or tendinosis)
  • Bursitis
  • Muscle strain or muscle tear
  • Muscle pain, joint pain or stiffness
  • Muscle imbalance
  • Fall prevention
